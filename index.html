The Simon Game

The Simon game, invented by Ralph H. Baer and Howard J. Morrison, was first released in 1978 by Milton Bradley (now owned by Hasbro). It quickly became a popular electronic game and has remained a classic ever since.

Who is "Simon" ?
"Simon" typically refers to the electronic device itself rather than a specific individual. The name "Simon" was chosen by the creators of the game, Ralph H. Baer and Howard J. Morrison, and it has since become synonymous with the game itself.

Rules

  1. Components:
    • The Simon game typically consists of a round electronic device with four colored buttons: red, green, blue, and yellow. Each button corresponds to a specific color and emits a distinct sound when pressed.
  2. Gameplay:
    • The game starts with Simon lighting up one or more buttons in a random sequence accompanied by their respective sounds.
    • The player must then repeat the sequence by pressing the buttons in the same order.
    • If the player correctly repeats the sequence, Simon adds a new random color to the end of the sequence and repeats the pattern.
    • The game continues in this manner, with Simon progressively increasing the length of the sequence, until the player makes a mistake.
  3. Mistakes:
    • Making a mistake in repeating the sequence, such as pressing the wrong button or pressing the buttons out of order, results in the game ending.
    • Simon signals a mistake by producing a specific sound and light sequence, and the player's score is displayed.
